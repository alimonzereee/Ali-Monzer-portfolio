## Overview

This project covers the simulation of both single-phase and three-phase voltage source inverters (VSI) using **Sinusoidal Pulse Width Modulation (SPWM)**. SPWM is the industry-standard modulation technique for producing high-quality AC output from a DC source, and is fundamental to variable frequency drives (VFDs), grid-tied inverters, and renewable energy power conversion systems.

## Sinusoidal PWM Principle

SPWM generates switching signals by comparing a sinusoidal reference waveform (at the desired output frequency) with a high-frequency triangular carrier waveform. When the reference exceeds the carrier, the upper switch conducts; otherwise, the lower switch conducts. This produces a PWM output whose fundamental frequency component is the desired AC sine wave.

**Key parameters:**
- **Modulation index (ma)** — Ratio of reference to carrier amplitude; controls output voltage magnitude
- **Frequency ratio (mf)** — Ratio of carrier to reference frequency; affects harmonic spectrum
- **Total Harmonic Distortion (THD)** — Primary quality metric of the output waveform

## Single-Phase Full-Bridge Inverter

The single-phase H-bridge (four-switch full bridge) was simulated with bipolar and unipolar SPWM schemes:

- **Bipolar SPWM**: Two switch pairs operate complementarily; simple but produces higher ripple
- **Unipolar SPWM**: Switches operate with phase-shifted carriers; reduces output voltage ripple and improves THD
- Output filtered with an LC low-pass filter to extract the fundamental sine wave

## Three-Phase Inverter

The three-phase voltage source inverter (six-switch bridge) was simulated with three-phase SPWM:

- Three reference sinusoids 120° phase-shifted, compared to a common triangular carrier
- Line-to-line output voltages analyzed for THD and harmonic content
- Suitable for three-phase AC motor drives and grid interconnection

## Simulation Results

Both inverter topologies were fully simulated in **PLECS** and **MATLAB/Simulink**:
- Gate signal waveforms for all switches
- Phase voltage and line-to-line voltage waveforms
- FFT spectrum analysis showing harmonic distribution
- THD comparison at different modulation indices and carrier frequency ratios
- Filter design for target THD specification

## Technologies Used

- **PLECS** — Primary switched-mode power electronics simulation
- **MATLAB** — FFT analysis, THD calculations, and reference waveform generation
- **LTspice** — Cross-verification of single-phase circuit behavior

## Key Takeaways

Increasing the frequency ratio (higher carrier frequency) shifts harmonics to higher frequencies, making output filtering easier and reducing filter component size — at the cost of higher switching losses. The three-phase topology achieves inherently lower THD than single-phase due to harmonic cancellation in the balanced system.
